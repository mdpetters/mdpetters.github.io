Development and Rigorous Validation of Modules for Predicting Organic Aerosol CCN Activity from Molecular Properties


Amorphous phase diagram of 100 nm sucrose aerosol

This DOE sponsored project focuses on validation of functional group approaches to model aerosol viscosity (Rothfuss and Petters, 2017, ES&T) and CCN activity (Petters et al., 2016, GMD). Special focus is on the potential influence of the amorphous particle phase state on cloud droplet activation. This effort is a collaboration between our group, the Kreidenweis reserach group, and the Ziemann research group.

New Studies Linking Ice Nucleation and Precipitation


Picodrops and nanodrops on an oil immersion freezing cold stage

This NSF sponsored project is focused on characterizing the properties of ice nuclei in rainwater. It centers around a new instrument (NC State cold-stage supported droplet freezing assay) to quantify the concentration of immersion mode freezing nuclei in aqueous suspensions. The investigable temperature range is [0,-36] °C, the investigable droplet diameters range is [50, 1000] µm, and the maximum investigable residence time on the cold stage is 1 week. The quantifyable concentration range is [1E4, 1E10] L-1 water. Ambient freezing nuclei concentrations can be obtained via particle-into-liquid sampling systems with a lower limit of detection of ~ 0.8 L-1 air.
